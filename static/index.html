HOW DO RATINGS DISTRIBUTE ACROSS GENRES?

IMDb shows audience preferences while Metascore reflects critics' opinions. Both form normal distributions, but Metascore tends to be more critical. Drama consistently receives the highest audience ratings.

AVERAGE IMDb AND METASCORE BY GENRE

Horror films consistently receive the lowest scores, while historical films, biographies, and dramas tend to receive the highest ratings from both critics and audiences.

HOW DO AUDIENCE AND CRITIC RATINGS ALIGN?

IMDb and Metascore are closely aligned (correlation: 0.72), with profitable films often rated higher. However, box office doesn't always reflect artistic success, as some critically acclaimed films perform modestly at the box office.

BUDGET VS BOX OFFICE

Animation and Adventure films lead with ROIs of 3.8x and 3.2x respectively on mid-range budgets ($80-120M). Action films command the highest budgets (avg. $185M) but achieve moderate 2.1x returns, while Drama and Horror films operate efficiently on smaller budgets ($15-25M) with ROIs ranging from 2.5-3.0x.

HOW HAVE IMDb RATINGS CHANGED OVER TIME?

The declining trend in IMDb ratings over time suggests either audiences have become more discerning in their expectations, or the overall thoughtfulness in film production has decreased, with a focus on quick returns over artistic merit.

WHICH DECADE HAD THE MOST HITS?

Films from earlier decades consistently score higher on IMDb, suggesting either a nostalgic bias in ratings or that these surviving classics have stood the test of time, representing the best of their era.

BUDGET VS BOX OFFICE

Most films fall into the category where box office is less than 2x the budget, but high-budget hits (>$100M) often achieve significant returns.
